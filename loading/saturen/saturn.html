Saturn is the second largest planet in the solar system after Jupiter. Saturn is a giant ball of gas. The density of this planet is less than water. A full day on Saturn is equal to 10 hours and 39 minutes on Earth and a year is equal to 10,767.5 days on Earth. Like Jupiter, the mass of Saturn is a gas, most of which consists of hydrogen gas. Saturn has the most moons among the planets of the solar system, the largest of which is called Titan. The rings of Saturn are located at a distance of 11,200 km. The rings of Saturn are composed of pieces of ice, rock and dust.
